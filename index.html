About Me!

Aloha, I am a 20-year-old graduate student at the University of Washington, Tacoma, currently studying to recieve my Masters of Science degree in Geospatial Technologies. Alongside, I am working for the City of Renton as a GIS analyst, Transportation Engineering Intern in the Public Works, Transportation Planning Division. At this position, I am continually learning and using GIS skills to create maps and databases that assist the city in making data-driven decisions.
Previously in May 2022, I recieved my Bachelor of Science degree in Geology at the University of Hawai'i, Hilo. At this university, I gained introductory and geology based GIS skills using ESRI applications. Following this course, I worked as a Geology Research Assistant where I lead a team in creating technology-based work products. From GIS analyzation, to data entry, our research focused on finding tephra deposits on the Big Island of Hawai'i that had agricultural-grade black cinder. Additionally I got the opportunity to be apart of Crack team, a group at UH-Hilo that worked in tandem with the United States Geological Survey, Hawaiian Volcanoes Observatory to conduct geodetic research about the islands deformation.
Born and raised in Kent, Washington, I previously attended Highline College and Kent-Meridian High School. Through the Running Start program, I was able to recieve my High School Diploma in tandem to my Associate of Arts degree in Geology, in June 2020.

Final 503 Project

Capstone

As the world erupts, floods, and finds ways to change just under our feet, communities find themselves underprepared due to the lack of hazard awareness. Being aware of your surrounding hazards allows for an understanding in how to be prepared, how to respond when a particular hazard occurs, and how to recover based off the hazard intensity. By using GIS resources, hazard assessment datasets, and historical hazardous events, I want to create an application that allows community members to insert their location onto a map and learn about the hazards that could affect their life. Helping build this basic understanding of hazard awareness allows the community to move forward in creating a plan to be better prepared for their future.
